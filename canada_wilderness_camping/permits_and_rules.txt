**Permits and Rules for Camping in Ontario**

In Ontario, backcountry camping is allowed in provincial parks and crown land, but different rules apply:

- **Provincial Parks**: You need a permit. Reservations are made through the Ontario Parks website.
- **Crown Land**: Canadian residents can camp for free up to 21 days in one location. Non-residents require a permit.
- **Fire Bans**: Always check current fire bans on the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources site.
- **Waste**: Pack out all garbage. No trace camping is encouraged.
- **Alcohol**: Permitted in many areas, but prohibited during certain holidays in provincial parks.
